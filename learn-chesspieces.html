Chess Pieces

Every chess piece has its own unique capabilites. To be a better chess player you must understand their purpose. Here we will learn the movement, starting postition and additional information about chess pieces

Here are the six different chess pieces:

  • Pawn
  • Rook
  • Knight
  • Bishop
  • Queen
  • King

Pawn

The pawn is a basic piece in chess and each player starts with eight pawns. Each pawns worth 1 point. The starting position of the pawns are on the second rank which is in front of the minor and major pieces.

Review

The pawn is the weakest piece on the game, but in the opening part it is very important. It is used to control the central squares. Later on the pawn will serve as a supporting piece. It is very important in a game because a player can win with only one pawn advantage

Movement

  1. The pawn may move forward to unoccupied square and up to two square on its first move
  2. It may capture diagonally in front of adjacent file if it is occupied by opponent's piece
  3. En passant' capture is applicable only if a pawn attacking square is passed by the opponents pawn which has advanced two squares
  4. When the pawn reaches the last rank, it must be promoted to a new piece. It may be a new rook, knight, bishop or a queen
  5. Pawn is the only piece that can't move backwards.

Rook

The rook is major piece in the game each players starts with two rooks each. Rook is only outranked by the queen and it is worth 5 points. The starting position of the rook is in the four corners of the board

Review

The rook is a strong piece it commonly used to control open and semi-open files. This piece is favorable on the end game and weak on early stage of the game. End games are more likely to be an open position so it control a lot of squares.

Movement

  1. The rook can move any number of squares horizontally and vertically.
  2. Can't jump over a piece

Knight

The knight is a minor piece piece in the game and each player has two knights. The starting position of the knight is on b1/g1 for white and b8/g8 for black. The knight is worth 3 points and it is considered as strong as bishop

Review

The knight is a complex piece in the game and its movement is unique. Knights are slightly better from bishop on closed position and slightly worse during open games. Knights are better if they control a lot of squares. So it is better if placed on the middle of the board and it is not ideal on the edge of the board.

Movement

  1. The knight is the only piece that can jump over another piece.
  2. It may move to the nearest square which stands but not on the same rank, file or diagonal. Some thought of it as an L pattern.

Bishop

The bishop is a minor piece in the game and each player starts with two bishops. The first bishop control the light squares and the other control the dark squares. It is located at c1/f1 for white and c8/f8 for black. The bishop is worth 3 points the same as the knight

Review

Bishops control long diagonals in the board. It is a good attacking and defending piece. A pair of bishop works together if they are not blocked. They can cover the entire board if placed in an active square.

Movement

  1. Bishops moves diagonally and never leave the color of it's square.
  2. Can't jump over a piece

Queen

Queens are the most powerful piece on the board. Each player starts with one queen and it is located on d1 square for white and d3 square for black. Queen is a major piece like the rook and it is worth 9 points.

Review

Since queen is a powerful piece avoid to move it early in the game so it can't be attack by weaker pieces. Try to bring your queen to a safe square and controls many squares.

Movement

  1. Queen moves the same as bishops and rooks.

  2. It can move many squares but can't jump over a piece.

King

King may not be as powerful as the queen but it is the most important piece on the board. When you lose your king you also lost the game. Each player can only have one king in the game. This piece is located on e1 for white and e8 for black.

Review

Bring your king to safety in the beggining of the game. The best strategy is to castle before attacking your opponent. In the later part of the game king can be a powerful piece and it can oppose the king of the other player.

Movement

  1. The king can one square in every direction.
  2. The king should move if it is in check
  3. It can't move to square that is being attacked by enemy piece.
  4. Castling is a single move executed by two pieces. It transfers two squares towards the rook and the rook transfers beside the king.
  5. You can only castly if there are no pieces between the two pieces. And the king is not under check or the square that the king must pass over is controlled by an opponent's piece.

Rules of Chess

Chess is a gamed played by two players and certain rules must be followed in order to play correctly. These rules are created by FIDE(International Chess Federation).
